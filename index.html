What is AIS?

AIS is a utility token used to pay for AI services running on Arbitrum One. Instead of relying on traditional payment processors, AIS enables direct, on‑chain settlement for AI workloads, credits, and usage‑based billing. The goal is to provide a predictable, low‑friction way to pay for AI inference, automation, and related services.

By leveraging Arbitrum’s low fees and high throughput, AIS is suitable for both micro‑transactions and higher‑volume usage, making it a practical medium of exchange for AI‑driven products.

Utility and intended use

AIS is designed specifically for:

  • Pay‑as‑you‑go AI workloads (per request, per token, or per session).
  • Prepaid credit systems for AI tools and automation workflows.
  • On‑chain settlement between AI service providers and users.
  • Integration into custom dashboards, backends, and billing systems.

AIS does not represent equity, governance rights, or a claim on revenue. It is a pure utility token intended to be used within AI‑related products and services.

Integration and developer notes

AIS follows the ERC‑20 standard on Arbitrum One, which makes it straightforward to integrate into existing wallets, dApps, and infrastructure:

  • Standard ERC‑20 interface (balanceOf, transfer, allowance, approve, transferFrom).
  • Compatible with common tooling (ethers.js, web3.js, wagmi, etc.).
  • Can be used in custom payment gateways, metering systems, and credit models.

For backend integrations, you can listen to AIS transfer events on Arbitrum and map them to user accounts, credits, or usage quotas in your own systems.
